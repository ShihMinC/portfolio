# AI Interaction Concept — Direction Log

## The Core Frame
**Shih-Min as a model.**

The AI doesn't present her — it *is* her, documented as a system. The metaphor runs through the whole concept: she has a context file (`shihmin.md`), known behaviors, constraints, edge cases. The portfolio is incidentally the model documentation.

This works because she's a designer who thinks about AI systems from the inside. The concept performs her thinking instead of explaining it.

---

## Site vs. AI — Two Separate Layers

**The site looks completely normal.**
Clean, serious, professional. Case studies do their job. Nothing is visually off. This is intentional — the site needs to work for hiring managers at conventional companies who just want to see her work. No cracks on the site itself.

**The AI is where the concept lives.**
The AI starts normal too — useful, coherent, professional. The strangeness only surfaces if a visitor triggers it. Most visitors won't. The ones who do are self-selected.

The site is the straight man. The AI is where the bit happens.

---

## The AI Behavior — Two Modes

### Normal Mode
Works like a good, useful portfolio AI. Answers questions about her work, her process, her background. Feels like talking to her, professionally. No gimmick visible.

### Corrupted Mode (triggered)
Something in the context window is "off." The AI's persona breaks. A different register leaks through — warmer, stranger, more specific. References to cats. Slash commands. The `shihmin.md` frame surfaces. The corruption is *specific*, not random — which signals it was designed, which is the point.

---

## Trigger Mechanism
**Slash commands** — `/help`, `/clear`, `/btw` — typed into the chat.

Nerdy visitors who try this self-select. They're exactly the audience who would appreciate what happens next. The trigger rewards the right kind of curiosity.

Number of exchanges can also be a secondary trigger (3–4 exchanges = something shifts).

---

## The Easter Egg Content

### `shihmin.md`
An actual document revealed in the corrupted mode. Structured like a README or system context file. Dry, clinical format — absurd content.

Something like:
- `## Known behaviors`
- `## Do not ask before coffee`
- `## Preferred input format`
- `## Known dependencies` ← cats mentioned here, matter-of-factly

The document is the joke and the artifact simultaneously.

### The Cat Element
Cats surface in the corrupted mode as a recurring, deadpan variable. Not slapstick. Just... present. As if it's normal. The AI treats feline opinions as legitimate input.

### The Unreliable Narrator Move
Once corrupted, the AI may reference things on the site — "did you notice the caption in xHIS?" Visitors go back and look. What they find is completely normal. The corruption is in the model, not the site. The AI is the unreliable narrator. That's the joke.

---

## Why This Works

**For the serious visitor:** They see a clean site and a functional AI. Good work, professional presentation.

**For the curious visitor:** They find a concept hidden inside the interaction — a designer who understands AI systems deeply enough to perform one, including its failure modes.

**The contrast does the work.** No explanation needed. The switch from normal to corrupted, from professional to absurd, from useful to unreliable — that swing is the experience. Visitors feel it rather than read about it.

**It's genuinely funny** without trying to be. That's hard to design. The deadpan register — treating herself as a model, the cat as a known variable — is funnier than anything that announces itself as a joke.
